Spaces System

Spaces replace channels: modes, linked Spaces, unlock conditions, and dynamic visibility.

Overview

Spaces are contextual, dynamic containers for activity — not static lists. Each Space can change visibility based on unlock conditions, link to other Spaces, and support multiple Modes. This allows Cities to shape narrative progression and control access without brittle permission matrices.

  • Mode-aware Spaces
  • Linked Spaces and dynamic flow
  • Unlock conditions and progressive visibility

How it fits in Nexus OS

Spaces are the primary surface for user activity in Nexus — they host Mini‑Apps, RP encounters, streams, and Studio sessions while reporting presence and events to the core systems.
